# 00 — Vision

**Superfans — Where Sports Fans Become Legends**

---

## Mission
Turn every sports fan into a creator.

## Vision
Build the world's largest sports intelligence network.

## Long-term goal
Become the **Bloomberg of Sports Attention** — the definitive system of record for what fans think, predict, and believe about every match on earth.

---

## Company narrative

Three billion people follow sports. They watch, argue, predict, and obsess — yet the entire emotional and intellectual output of that fandom evaporates the moment a match ends. There is no record of who called it right. No reputation for the fan who saw the upset coming. No way for the brilliant amateur analyst to be discovered, followed, or paid.

The incumbents each own one slice and stop there. **Sofascore** owns the data but not the conversation. **Twitter/X** owns the conversation but not the structure. **Reddit** owns the community but not the identity. **Polymarket** owns prediction but gates it behind money and risk. **Patreon/Substack** own creator monetization but know nothing about sports.

Superfans fuses these into one product: a sports social network where **knowledge is the currency and accuracy is the status symbol.** Fans predict matches not for money but for reputation. Every prediction is scored, every fan accumulates a verifiable track record, and the best minds rise into creators with audiences and revenue. AI sits underneath, turning raw match data into previews, sentiment, and insight that make every visit worth it.

We are not building a sportsbook. We are building the **identity and reputation layer for global sports fandom** — and on top of it, a creator economy and a data network that compounds with every prediction submitted.

---

## Why this matters
- **Fandom is unmonetized intelligence.** Billions of hours of analysis are produced daily and captured by no one.
- **Betting extracts; reputation compounds.** Money-based prediction is zero-sum and regulated into a corner. Reputation-based prediction is positive-sum, globally legal, and infinitely scalable.
- **Creators have no sports-native home.** A sports analyst today must duct-tape Twitter + Substack + Discord + a spreadsheet. We collapse that stack.
- **Data is the endgame.** Aggregate fan prediction and sentiment is a proprietary dataset no incumbent can replicate, valuable to media, teams, and operators.

---

## Future state (the product in 5 years)
A fan opens Superfans before every match. The feed shows predictions from analysts they trust, AI previews tuned to their teams, and live community debate. They submit a one-tap prediction; their accuracy score updates when the whistle blows. The top 1% of predictors run paid channels, prediction leagues, and communities with thousands of subscribers. Media companies license our sentiment index. Clubs watch our community dashboards to understand their fans. Superfans is the place sports happens *between* the matches.

---

## Long-term strategic moat
1. **Reputation graph** — a per-user, cryptographically auditable accuracy history that cannot be ported elsewhere. The longer you're on Superfans, the more your status is worth.
2. **Creator lock-in** — creators bring their audiences; audiences can't be exported with their reputation intact.
3. **Proprietary data** — daily prediction + sentiment volume that no competitor can backfill.
4. **Network density** — communities, follows, and leagues that get more valuable as they grow.
5. **AI advantage** — models fine-tuned on our own outcome-labeled prediction data improve as the network grows.

---

## Network effects
- **Direct social:** more fans → richer feed, debate, and communities → more reason to join.
- **Creator ↔ audience:** creators attract fans; fans attract creators chasing audience.
- **Data flywheel:** more predictions → better AI → better insight → more engagement → more predictions.
- **Reputation gravity:** the longer your track record, the higher the cost of leaving — switching means starting your status from zero.

## Founder story
Superfans began with a simple, maddening observation by its founders: the smartest person in every group chat — the friend who *always* calls the upset — has nothing to show for it. No record, no audience, no reward. Meanwhile betting apps were happy to take that same person's money and give them nothing but risk in return. The founding team, builders from sports, community, and consumer product, set out to build the opposite of a sportsbook: a place where being *right* is the prize, where reputation is portable status, and where the best fans become creators with real audiences and income. Superfans is the platform they wished existed for the friend who always knew.
